Hello there! I am Jordan, a data science student, musician, and Master of Information candidate attending Rutgers University School of Communication and Information. I currently have a B.A.in Music Performance and Music Education from Rutgers Mason Gross School of the Arts.
As a teacher and leader, I like to lead by example and promote diverse ways of thinking to my students. I apply this to my own learning, as I would not have been able to make the jarring pivot from the field of performing arts to technology and statistics.
Academically, I enjoy taking courses such as Problem Solving with Data, Web Development, Machine Learning, and Information Behaviors. One of my term projects utilized the Spotify API to pull song data and conduct an analysis on music taste preference using techniques such as principal component analysis and t-SNE to visualize correlations between songs and their properties. I also trained a convolutional neural network to identify and classify music from audio samples with a 69.4% accuracy.
I have been working at RMC Studios since 2017, where I work with the studio to teach students as a music teacher and help organize events such as open-mic nights, concerts showcasing students’ skills, and work as a stage crew for setting up/tearing down lighting and sound equipment.
When I am not working or doing data science projects, I am arranging music, playing cello, drawing, or trying to learn a new language.
