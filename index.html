Hello there! I am Jordan, a data science student, musician, and Master of Information candidate attending Rutgers University School of Communication and Information. I currently have a B.A. in Music Performance and Music Education from Rutgers Mason Gross School of the Arts.
As a teacher and leader, I like to lead by example and promote diverse ways of thinking to my students. I apply this to my own learning as I would not have been able to make the jarring pivot from the field of performing arts and education to technology and data analysis.
I enjoy taking courses such as Problem Solving with Data, Web Development, Machine Learning, and Information Behaviors. One of my term projects used the Spotify API to pull song data and conduct an analysis on music taste preference using techniques such as principal component analysis and t-SNE to visualize correlations between songs and their properties. I also trained a convolutional neural network to identify and classify music by genre from audio samples with a 69.4% accuracy.
I have been working as an intern with Penn Medicine as a Data Analyst in what started as the Business Development Office. My work projects and credits at Penn Medicine include: obtaining and cleaning contact information data for Penn Medicine's "Cellicon Valley '23", yielding over $50k in savings for Penn. I have also conducted retrieval and analysis for the Faculty Database of Penn's Environmental Innovations Initiative, for which I am thanked on the bottom of the page.
When I am not working or doing data science projects, I am usually out in the afternoons teaching private music lessons or playing in musicals or concerts throughout New Jersey! If you've ever attended a production company's show in New Jersey, chances are you might have heard me play!
